THE REAPPEARANCE OF RACHEL PRICE

mystery / crime · review

the reappearance of rachel price follows teenager bel price, whose mother, rachel, vanished without a trace sixteen years earlier. bel was the only witness to whatever happened that night, which is why everyone has always turned to her for answers — except that she was far too young at the time to remember anything of use. when a true crime documentary revisits the famous cold case, rachel suddenly and shockingly reappears alive, claiming she was kidnapped and held against her will for the entirety of those sixteen years. while everyone around bel is overjoyed and ready to welcome rachel back with open arms, bel cannot shake the feeling that something is deeply wrong. certain parts of her mother's story simply do not add up, and the more she digs, the more unsettling the picture becomes. what follows is a gripping unraveling of shocking secrets, hidden truths and carefully constructed lies that forces bel — and the reader — to question everything and everyone, revealing a truth far more complicated and far darker than anyone could have imagined. a brilliantly plotted thriller that keeps you second guessing until the very last page.

ALL THE BRIGHT PLACES

rom com · personal thoughts

this book has a tender, beating heart unlike almost anything else i have read, and it made me feel everything — laughter, warmth, hope and an absolute flood of tears, sometimes within the same chapter. finch is one of the most memorable characters i have ever encountered in fiction — brilliantly written, achingly funny, wildly alive in every scene he inhabits, and deeply, heartbreakingly human beneath all of it. the way he loved violet was something else entirely. it was not loud or performative — it was quiet and devoted and so completely genuine that every small gesture he made toward her felt enormous. the word yearn does not even begin to cover it. he saw her, truly saw her, at a time when she could barely see herself, and that kind of love written that honestly is genuinely rare. and then the ending came. i am not going to say a word about what happens because you deserve to experience it completely unprepared, the way i did — but i will say that i cried in a way that felt necessary, like the book had been building toward that release all along and it could not have ended any other way. an absolute ten out of ten. a book i will carry with me for a very long time.

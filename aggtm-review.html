A GOOD GIRL'S GUIDE TO MURDER

review

a very well written mystery that manages to keep you on your toes from start to finish. the plot is layered and intelligent, with twists that feel genuinely unconventional — not the kind you see coming from a mile away, but the kind that reframe everything you thought you knew. what makes it stand out is that the twists don't feel cheap or forced; they are carefully woven into the story and build on each other in a way that feels both surprising and inevitable in hindsight. holly jackson has a real talent for misdirection, and the way she plants clues throughout makes it incredibly rewarding to look back and connect the dots once everything unravels.

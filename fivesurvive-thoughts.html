FIVE SURVIVE

mystery / crime · personal thoughts

five survive was a gripping, dark thriller that had me completely invested from the very first chapter and never once loosened its grip. the suspense was relentless — the kind that sits heavy in your chest and refuses to let you put the book down, even when you know you should sleep. what holly jackson does so brilliantly here is create an atmosphere of total claustrophobia and dread that feels suffocating in the best possible way. you are trapped in that rv with those characters, feeling every spike of panic and every agonizing moment of doubt right alongside them. while the story did not always unfold the way i anticipated, and there were a handful of plot developments that stretched believability just slightly, none of it ever took away from how thoroughly entertaining and addictive the whole experience was. the way she makes you second guess every single character is masterful — just when you think you have figured out who to trust, she pulls the rug out from under you again. it is fast, it is tense, it is dark, and it is exactly the kind of thriller that reminds you why you love the genre. absolutely one to recommend to anyone who enjoys high stakes psychological suspense.

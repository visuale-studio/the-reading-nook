TO ALL THE BOYS I'VE LOVED BEFORE

rom com · personal thoughts

iconic is truly the only word for it. the fact that this series captured so many hearts that netflix turned it into not one but three films? completely deserved and entirely iconic. the fake dating trope between lara jean and peter kavinsky is one that has been done countless times across romance fiction, and yet somehow jenny han made it feel completely fresh and utterly irresistible. every single interaction between them had me swooning — the little moments, the stolen glances, the slow and painful realization that what started as pretend had quietly become something very, very real. and when they finally admitted their feelings for each other? absolute meltdown. genuinely. it felt like it took the entire universe conspiring to get them there, but when it happened it was so worth every agonizing page of buildup. like honestly — it was about time! the fake dating concept may seem predictable going in, but the way it was carried out made every beat feel earned and every emotion feel completely genuine. this series has no right to be as good as it is, and yet here we are.

ANGELS AND DEMONS

mystery / crime · review

angels and demons is a breathless, high stakes thriller that plunges you headfirst into one of history's most enduring and controversial conflicts — the ancient war between science and religion. at its centre is robert langdon, harvard symbologist, who is pulled into a race against time alongside vittoria vetra, a brilliant physicist reeling from the brutal murder of her father — a man who was at once a deeply devout believer in god and one of the most respected scientists of his generation. the investigation leads them deep into the shadowy and long rumoured world of the illuminati, a secret brotherhood whose vendetta against the catholic church stretches back centuries, and whose resurgence threatens to unleash catastrophic consequences on the heart of the vatican itself. dan brown orchestrates the chaos with remarkable control — the plot moves at an almost relentless pace, ricocheting between ancient churches, hidden passageways and the corridors of power with a propulsive energy that makes it nearly impossible to put down. what elevates it beyond a standard thriller is the richness of the world it inhabits — the tension between faith and reason, the weight of centuries of history, and the genuine moral complexity at the heart of the story all combine to make angels and demons a book that is as intellectually stimulating as it is gripping.

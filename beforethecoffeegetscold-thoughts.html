BEFORE THE COFFEE GETS COLD

rom com · personal thoughts

i would be lying if i said i did not cry at least once while reading this series. genuinely, truly cry — not the kind of polite watery eyes you get from a sad film, but the kind that creeps up on you quietly and catches you completely off guard because you did not expect a book about a small cafe and cups of coffee to reach so far inside your chest. feeling real, unfiltered emotion through a book is something that has become increasingly rare, and this series achieved it effortlessly and repeatedly. what is so beautiful about it is its simplicity. there is no flair, no unnecessary drama, no complex plot twists or grand gestures — just people. ordinary, flawed, deeply human people who want nothing more than to go back and relive their most precious moments, to say the things they never said, to hold on a little longer to someone they lost. that simplicity is not a weakness — it is the entire point, and it is executed with such gentleness and emotional intelligence that it leaves a mark on you long after the last page. this series reminded me why i love reading in the first place.

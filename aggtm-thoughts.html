A GOOD GIRL'S GUIDE TO MURDER

personal thoughts

while the premise is genuinely interesting and the overall concept is one that hooked me from the start, there were moments throughout where the story felt a little stretched — almost as though the author wasn't entirely sure what direction to take certain sections and filled the space with scenes that didn't add much to the overall narrative. it occasionally disrupted the pacing in a way that was hard to ignore. that said, the moments where everything clicked were satisfying in the best way, and there is something deeply gratifying about watching justice unfold, even if the journey there had its bumps. overall, it was not quite my cup of tea, but i can absolutely see why so many people love it — it is a solid mystery with a lot going for it, just not entirely for me personally.

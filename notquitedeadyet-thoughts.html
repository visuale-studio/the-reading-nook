NOT QUITE DEAD YET

mystery / crime · personal thoughts

not quite dead yet was a gutpunch of a book that i was not remotely prepared for. from the very first twist to the very last, it kept me completely on my toes — every time i thought i had figured out where the story was going, it took a sharp and unexpected turn that left me reeling. holly jackson has a truly extraordinary talent for building suspense and layering revelations in a way that feels both shocking and perfectly constructed in hindsight. but what made this book hit so much harder than a typical thriller was the emotion running beneath every page. this was not just a mystery — it was a deeply human story about time, regret and the people who matter most to us, and it carried that weight beautifully. i will be honest — i was absolutely heartbroken near the end of the book when jet passes before she gets to find out who killed her. it was one of those moments that genuinely stopped me in my tracks, the kind that sits with you for days afterward. it is rare for a thriller to make you feel that deeply, and the fact that this one did so effortlessly is a testament to just how well crafted it truly is. an emotional rollercoaster from beginning to end — wonderful, devastating, and completely unforgettable.

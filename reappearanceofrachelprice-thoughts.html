THE REAPPEARANCE OF RACHEL PRICE

mystery / crime · personal thoughts

the reappearance of rachel price was an addictive and thoroughly entertaining read overall, though i will admit it took a little while to fully get its hooks into me. the opening chapters — structured around documentary interviews and behind the scenes footage — felt a little slow to get through, and i was not completely gripped in those early pages. it felt like the story was still finding its footing, and i had to push through that initial stretch on faith alone. but once the story truly got going, it became completely unputdownable. every chapter had me questioning what was real and who could be trusted, and the suspense built so steadily and so effectively that by the final act i was reading at a pace i could barely keep up with. holly jackson did a fantastic job of keeping me genuinely guessing throughout — i did not see many of the twists coming, and each revelation reframed everything i thought i knew in a way that felt both shocking and completely earned. the characters felt grounded and believable, and the pacing in the second half was relentless in the best possible way. overall, it is a brilliant thriller that is absolutely worth sticking with through those slower early chapters, and it has firmly cemented itself as one of my favourite mystery reads.

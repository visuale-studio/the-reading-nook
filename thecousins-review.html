THE COUSINS

mystery / crime · review

the cousins is a curious and quietly compelling mystery that is difficult to place neatly into any single category — and that is very much part of its charm. the plot is oddly constructed in a way that initially leaves you feeling slightly off balance, unsure of where the story is heading or who exactly you are supposed to be rooting for. yet somehow, that sense of disorientation works entirely in the book's favour. karen mcmanus has a distinctive gift for building a world that feels both familiar and deeply unsettling at the same time, and the execution of the storyline here is exquisite — layered, carefully paced, and far more intricate than it first appears. the mystery at the heart of it unravels gradually and with considerable skill, rewarding patience with revelations that reframe everything that came before. it is confusing in the very best sense of the word — the kind of confused that keeps you turning pages rather than putting the book down, constantly searching for the thread that will make everything click into place. an unconventional read that is far more absorbing than its premise might initially suggest.

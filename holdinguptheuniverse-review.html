HOLDING UP THE UNIVERSE

rom com · review

holding up the universe is a warm and deeply human story about two people who could not appear more different on the surface, and the quiet, unexpected magic that happens when they truly see each other. libby, who spent years confined to her home after her weight made it impossible to leave, is returning to school with a fierce determination to reclaim her life and her confidence on her own terms. jack, the popular and outwardly self assured jock who seems to have everything, is carrying a secret he has carefully hidden from everyone around him — a neurological condition that prevents him from recognising faces, even those of the people he loves most. what begins as an unlikely and somewhat fraught connection between them slowly, surely and sweetly blossoms into something neither of them anticipated — a relationship built not on first impressions or social currency, but on genuine understanding and hard won trust. jennifer niven writes both characters with enormous tenderness and respect, never reducing either of them to their circumstances, and the result is a love story that feels truly earned. a beautiful reminder that the people who see us most clearly are often the ones we least expected.

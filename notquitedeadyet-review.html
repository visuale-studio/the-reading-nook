NOT QUITE DEAD YET

mystery / crime · review

not quite dead yet is a brilliantly unconventional thriller from holly jackson that takes a premise unlike anything else in the genre and runs with it with remarkable confidence. at the centre of it all is jet — a girl whose entire personality is built around putting things off until later. she is a chronic procrastinator in the truest sense, someone who has always believed there will be more time. that belief is violently shaken when a murder attempt leaves her in a deeply unusual position: partially alive, with a doctor's grim assessment giving her exactly one week before her body gives out entirely. rather than spending that week in grief or denial, jet — alongside her fiercely loyal best friend billy — channels everything she has into solving her own murder before her time runs out. what follows is a race against the clock that is equal parts tense mystery and deeply emotional character study. holly jackson manages to balance the urgency of the investigation with the weight of jet's situation in a way that feels genuinely moving rather than gimmicky. it is dark, it is clever, it is unlike anything you have read before, and it will stay with you long after the final page.

THE COUSINS

mystery / crime · personal thoughts

i will be straightforward — the cousins was not karen mcmanus at her best, and if you have read one of us is lying or two can keep a secret before picking this up, you may find yourself feeling that same slight sense of disappointment that i did. it was by no means a bad book — it was readable, it was engaging in stretches, and there were moments where the mystery genuinely pulled me in. but it never quite reached the heights that karen mcmanus is clearly capable of, and that gap between what it was and what it could have been was hard to ignore. certain sections of the book felt genuinely unnecessary — scenes and subplots that seemed to pad the story out without meaningfully contributing to it, leaving you with the nagging feeling that a tighter edit would have served it far better. it is the kind of book you finish and feel fairly satisfied with in the moment, but that fades from memory relatively quickly because it never fully delivered on the intrigue it promised. good, but not great — and from an author this talented, good feels just a little bit like a missed opportunity.

ANGELS AND DEMONS

mystery / crime · personal thoughts

i loved this book — perhaps even more than i expected to. what struck me most deeply was the audacity with which dan brown chose to wade into territory that so many writers would shy away from entirely. the relationship between science and religion, the inner workings of the vatican, the very real historical existence of the illuminati — these are not comfortable or simple subjects, and yet brown handles them with a depth, intensity and genuine curiosity that never feels exploitative or sensationalist. rather than offering easy answers or taking obvious sides, he leans into the complexity of it all, and the result is a story that genuinely unravels a new world for the reader — one built on centuries of tension, belief, power and secrecy that most of us never pause to consider in our daily lives. once again, dan brown's signature approach of weaving meticulous historical research into a tightly wound crime narrative is on full display, and once again it works beautifully. there is something uniquely satisfying about a thriller that leaves you not just entertained but genuinely more informed and more curious about the world than you were before you started. angels and demons does exactly that, and it does it with extraordinary confidence and flair. a book that i finished feeling like i had genuinely learned something — and that is the highest compliment i can give.

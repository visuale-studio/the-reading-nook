TO ALL THE BOYS I'VE LOVED BEFORE

rom com · review

on the surface, to all the boys i've loved before follows a plot we have all seen before — the popular, charming jock and the quiet, introverted girl — but jenny han takes that familiar framework and elevates it into something genuinely outstanding. what could have been a predictable, paint by numbers romance becomes a deeply emotional and beautifully observed story that has you swooning over every single page. a huge part of what makes the series so special is kitty, lara jean's mischievous younger sister, who is without question the unsung mvp of the entire trilogy. without her delightful meddling, none of it would have ever happened — and honestly, we owe her everything. each book in the series feels thoughtfully and carefully planned, with jenny han clearly knowing exactly where she wants to take lara jean's story and trusting the reader to follow. the writing puts you so completely inside lara jean's head that you don't just read her emotions — you feel them. every flutter, every doubt, every moment of joy and heartache lands with a realness that is rare in romance fiction and keeps you turning pages long after you should have gone to sleep.
